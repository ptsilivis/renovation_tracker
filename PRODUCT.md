# Product context — RenoHub

register: product

## Users

A small group of non-technical people (often a family) co-managing one or more
home renovations. They open the app to answer concrete questions: how much is
left in the budget, what is paid, which task is next, what did we decide about the
kitchen tiles. Mixed devices, mostly phones and laptops, in daylight, unhurried.
Bilingual Greek / English; the bundled sample project is a Greek stone-house
renovation in Mani.

## Product purpose

One shared, trustworthy source of truth for renovation money and activities,
replacing scattered chats and spreadsheets. Money accuracy and legibility matter
most: numbers must read cleanly and never feel like a flashy fintech pitch.

## Brand & tone

Calm, precise, grounded. The **Blueprint** palette: a confident architect's blue
(`#2b5bd7`) on cool near-white neutrals, with a restrained monochrome treatment of
data (over-budget reads as ink, under as muted grey — no traffic-light colour).
Commissioner (a clean geometric sans) for UI and headings; JetBrains Mono for every
figure so numbers align like a spec sheet. The feeling is a well-kept household
ledger drawn on drafting paper, not a dashboard demo.

## Anti-references

- Not a neon/gradient SaaS dashboard. No hero-metric template, no gradient text.
- Not cold corporate finance (navy + gold, dense fintech).
- Not playful/gamified. Restraint over decoration; delight lives in small,
  earned moments (a satisfying press, a smooth tab change), never on every pixel.

## Strategic principles

- Familiarity is a feature: standard top-nav + editable tables behave as expected.
- Every interactive control has default / hover / focus / active / disabled states.
- Motion conveys state only (feedback, reveal, tab change), 150–250 ms, ease-out.
- Legible money: tabular numerals, variance colored (over = terracotta, under = green).
- Bilingual EL/EN chrome; data stays in its source language.
